The 30% Cap Created Perverse Incentives

When only 30% of students can receive top grades regardless of performance, learning becomes a competition against peers rather than mastery of material.

  • Students avoided challenging courses to protect GPA
  • Collaboration suffered as classmates became competitors
  • Risk-taking in learning was systematically punished
  • Fudan students faced disadvantage vs. peer institutions with grade inflation

"Changing rules doesn't change minds."

I helped design for a static environment, ignoring the dynamic incentive loop where students would rationally game the system to minimize risk. You cannot change behavior by simply patching a rule; you must design mechanisms that are robust to strategic adaptation.

Goodhart's Law

When a Measure Becomes a Target

The PNP option was designed to reduce the cost of exploration. But once it existed, students optimized for it as a risk-reduction tool‚Äîexactly the opposite of its intended purpose.

In kernel optimization, hardware constraints are rigid but passive. In social systems, however, the agents are adversarial‚Äîthey actively adapt their strategies to exploit the rules.

The Pattern Repeats

  • Standardized tests ‚Üí Teaching to the test
  • Citation metrics ‚Üí Citation gaming
  • Engagement metrics ‚Üí Clickbait optimization
  • PNP for exploration ‚Üí PNP for avoidance

What This Project Taught Me

This experience crystallized a key insight: public articulation can build consensus, but institutional change requires institutional champions. And even successful policy change doesn't guarantee behavioral change. My ongoing role‚Äîcollecting data and providing feedback‚Äîreflects a shift from advocacy to empirical diagnosis.

Research Questions This Opens
